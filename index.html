🏌️ Welcome to Harbour Town

The RBC Heritage is back on Hilton Head Island — freshly renovated greens, the smallest targets on Tour, and a $20M purse with no cut. This is a shot-maker's paradise where bombers go to die and iron savants feast. Here's your cheat sheet.

01

🎯 Second-Shot Course

Four of the last five winners finished top-7 in SG: Approach during the tournament. Harbour Town's greens average just 3,700 sq ft — the second smallest on Tour. You can leave the driver in the bag and outscore the bombers. This is the week where precision beats power.

02

🐑 Fade the Masters Hangover

One of the sharpest DFS edges this week: players who were in contention at the Masters on Sunday are mentally and physically drained. Scheffler breaking this trend in 2024 was considered one of his most impressive wins ever. Target guys who missed the cut or weren't in the mix at Augusta — they're fresher and lower owned.

03

🚂 Follow the Sharp Money

We track odds from Sunday open through Tuesday. When a player's line shortens (e.g. +5200 → +4500), that's "steam" — sharp bettors are backing him. The touts won't catch this until Wednesday. You have it now. Combine steam with high fit and low ownership for the optimal GPP play.

04

🎰 Studs & Duds Strategy

No cut means your $6,000 guys get all four rounds. The expert consensus (Pat Mayo, Blickle, RotoWire) is unanimous: load 3 studs above $9k, then fill with high-upside values below $7k. The middle tier ($7.5k-$8.5k) is a trap — not enough ceiling, too much ownership. Let the optimizer find the edges.

📈 DFS Edge: PM Wave Advantage

The early AM wave faces the stiffest easterly wind off the Atlantic. By late morning the wind shifts SSE and drops to 5-10 mph. The PM wave (Scheffler, Schauffele, Fitzpatrick, Henley, Cantlay, Morikawa, Burns) gets significantly calmer conditions. However — those guys are also the highest owned. The counter-play: AM wave leverage plays (Straka, Gotterup, Min Woo Lee, Hovland) who handle wind with low ball flights.
